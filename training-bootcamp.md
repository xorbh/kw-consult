# AI Training Bootcamp

## The Wedge

The hardest part of selling AI services to non-technology-first companies is getting through the door. These organisations know AI matters but don't know where to start, don't trust vendor pitches, and aren't ready to commit to a transformation programme.

The AI Training Bootcamp solves this. It is a low-commitment, high-value engagement that gives us direct access to the people who actually do the work — and gives them practical skills they can use immediately.

It is also our primary discovery mechanism. Every bootcamp is a structured opportunity to understand how a company operates, where the friction is, and where AI can create real value.

## How It Works

### The Trainer

Each bootcamp is delivered by a trainer who can:

- Teach non-technical teams how to use AI tools effectively in their day-to-day roles
- Demystify the technology — strip away the hype and show what AI actually does well (and where it falls short)
- Run hands-on workshops where participants solve real problems from their own work using AI
- Adapt the content to the industry and roles in the room — a session for procurement looks different from one for finance or operations

The bootcamp is not a lecture series. It is practical, role-specific, and grounded in the participants' actual work.

### The Discovery Agent

Alongside the trainer, we deploy an AI agent that engages directly with teams before, during, and after the bootcamp.

**Before the bootcamp:**
- Conversational intake with participants to understand their role, daily tasks, tools they use, and where they spend the most time on repetitive or manual work
- Collects structured data on workflows, pain points, and information bottlenecks

**During the bootcamp:**
- Captures questions, use cases, and "what if" scenarios that participants raise
- Identifies patterns across teams — common frustrations, shared data dependencies, duplicated effort

**After the bootcamp:**
- Follow-up conversations to understand what participants tried, what stuck, and what they want to do next
- Produces a structured discovery report: mapped workflows, identified opportunities, estimated impact, and recommended next steps

The discovery agent does what would normally take weeks of consulting interviews — but it does it continuously, at scale, and in the participants' own words.

## Why It Works as a Go-To-Market Strategy

**Low barrier to entry**: A training bootcamp is an easy yes. It's a known format, a bounded commitment, and it delivers immediate value to the team regardless of what happens next.

**Builds trust from the inside out**: Instead of selling to the C-suite and hoping it trickles down, we start with the people who will actually use the AI. When they see the value, they become internal advocates.

**Discovery without the consulting price tag**: Traditional discovery engagements are expensive and slow. The bootcamp bundles discovery into a training engagement — we learn what we need to know while the client gets upskilled.

**Generates qualified pipeline**: Every bootcamp produces a discovery report full of specific, actionable opportunities. These aren't hypothetical — they're grounded in real conversations with the people who do the work. This makes the transition to a deployment engagement natural rather than forced.

**Scales across the organisation**: One bootcamp with operations leads to another with finance, then procurement, then HR. Each one deepens our understanding of the business and expands the opportunity.

```
Bootcamp Engagement
    ├── Trainer delivers practical AI skills
    ├── Discovery agent maps workflows and opportunities
    └── Produces discovery report
            │
            ▼
    Opportunity Assessment
    ├── Prioritised list of agent deployment candidates

    └── Recommended pilot scope
            │
            ▼
    Pilot Deployment
    ├── First agent deployed via AI Management Layer
    ├── Traced, monitored, and tuned
    └── Results measured against baseline
            │
            ▼
    Expansion
    ├── Additional agents across roles and departments
    ├── Managed service relationship established
    └── Compounding value from trace data and workflow intelligence
```

## What Makes This Different

- **Not just training**: Most AI training is generic and disconnected from the business. Ours is tailored to the roles in the room and doubles as a discovery exercise.
- **The agent does the heavy lifting on discovery**: Instead of relying solely on consultant interviews and workshops, the discovery agent captures information at scale and in context — producing richer, more honest insights.
- **Immediate value, long-term relationship**: Participants walk away with usable skills. The company walks away with a clear picture of where AI fits. We walk away with a qualified pipeline and deep understanding of the business.
